A map of Google Maps reviews removed from Berlin venues after defamation complaints, using § NetzDG.

How it works

Germany's NetzDG law requires platforms to remove flagged content within 24 hours or face fines up to €50 million. Defamation is covered. Legal services file complaints from €49. Google processes them. The reviewer often doesn't know.

The data

Google discloses removal counts in bands (1, 2–5, 6–10 … "over 250"). Counts cover the last 365 days and exclude appeals. All figures use the midpoint of each band. Google stops counting above 250 — those venues are a floor, not a total.

Caveats

Google's own disclosures. Nothing private. High counts mean complaints were filed and processed — not that a venue did anything wrong.
